Christian Mailer, born in 1905, worked as a pattern cutter for Robert, and then for Lucien until 1946, when he officially opened the Christian Couture House at 30 Obroud. Working for established names in fashion at the time meant Gore didn't have to "wait to find out" when he finally launched his brand—in fact, his grand opening was the long-awaited event that created a radical shift in fashion! The designs on display were so different from the current World War II fashion that the editor-in-chief of Harper's Bazaar at the time said, "This is quite a revolution, dear Christian! Your dresses have such a new look." "New Look" were two words used to describe fashion mailers for years afterward—the extreme emphasis on female forms in stark contrast to the post-war era.
